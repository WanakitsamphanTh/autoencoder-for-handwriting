Application of Autoencoders for Handwriting Evaluation
Thank you for your attention! We are students from National Institute of Technology Sendai College. We have developed this website for presentation in Thailand-Japan Student Science Fair 2023 and to ley everyone try the demo program we have developed.
How It Began
Sendai National Institute of Technology have a number of foreign students and interns from many countries, for example, Thailand, Malaysia, Mongolia, France, Finland etc. From our experience, many foreigners have been struggling to learning Japanese in various aspects. So we thought, as our school have many researches on information and technology, why don't we build something to help them learn to write Japanese.
The Idea Behind Our Project
We have studied many fields of the artificial intelligence to bring this project to life. We first have done

Handwritten Character Recognition Model for Foreignersâ€™ Handwritten Japanese

but finally we came up with the idea of using

autoencoder

to evaluate foreigners' handwriting, which we are very proud to present. The model gets handwriting images as its inputs and encode them into feature space. And again, from those encoded tensors, the model reconstructs the inputs as its outputs. While being trained, the model must learn how to minimalize losses between inputs and outputs. This is how the autoencoder technique works and what we utilized to evaluate handwriting We studied the difference of reconstruction loss between handwriting by Japanese natives which was carefully and well written and handwriting by foreigners which included incorrect handwriting, improper handwriting and correct handwriting in order to develop a tool to help study Japanese in the future.
